About Us


Founded in 1983, the Case Taekwondo Club is the oldest surviving martial arts club on campus. We are committed to preparing our members for the real world while addressing the morals and philosophy of the art that is Taekwondo. The mission of the Case Western Reserve University Taekwondo Club is two-fold:

  • To provide its members and instructors with the highest possible standards, values and environment for learning, practicing and instructing the martial arts.
  • To foster personal growth and development in club members as individuals, leaders, and community members through the physical and mental training inherent in Taekwondo.

The Taekwondo Club has historically been known for offering its members the chance to expand their training in various additional martial arts, including Judo and Hapkido. We are pleased to announce that the Taekwondo Club will be assisting the Judo Club in the 2015-2016 academic year by providing them a space to train this year. We invite our members to participate in Judo practices and open our doors to any Judo practitioners to train with the Taekwondo Club.
